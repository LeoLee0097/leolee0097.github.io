About Me

Introduction

Hello, my name is Sihao(Leo) Li. I am a PhD candidate with a passion for wireless sensor networks and Wi-Fi fingerprint indoor localization. With three years of experience in indoor localization, I have developed a deep understanding and expertise in Wi-Fi fingerprint based indoor positioning and navigation.

Throughout my career, I have had the opportunity to work on various projects and collaborate with talented individuals, which has enriched my skills and broadened my perspectives. I am constantly seeking new challenges and opportunities to expand my knowledge and contribute to innovative solutions.

My areas of expertise include [Wireless Sensor Networks, Unslotted CSMA in IEEE 802.15.4, Optimal Message Bundling, and COOJA Simu/Emulator]. Moreover, I am proficient in [Python Programming for Neural Networks] and have a strong understanding of [LaTex Paper Writing].

In addition to my technical skills, I am a strong communicator and enjoy working in collaborative environments. I believe in the power of teamwork and effective communication to drive successful outcomes.

I am excited to share my knowledge and experiences through this blog, where I will be discussing topics related to [Xi’an Jiaotong Liverpool University & Liverpool University MSc CAN 406/403 courses resources, Semi-Supervised Learning, and COOJA Simu/Emulator]. I hope you find my articles informative and engaging.

Thank you for visiting my blog and joining me on this journey of exploration and learning.
If you have any ideas or questions, please e-mail me at: leoworkspace@163.com.
